## Mission & strategy

- **Focus:** RAS-addicted cancers (~30% of human cancers) — **PDAC**, **NSCLC**, **CRC** priority tumor types.
- **Differentiation:** **RAS(ON)** tri-complex inhibitors targeting **GTP-bound active RAS**, vs first-gen **KRAS(OFF)** G12C inhibitors (Amgen sotorasib, BMS adagrasib).
- **Stage (mid-2026):** **Late-stage clinical** company pivoting toward commercial buildout — not early discovery startup.

### Preclinical themes (public science)

- Resistance to G12C(OFF) inhibitors
- Adaptive **wt RAS** signaling
- **RAS(ON) doublets**
- CNS penetration
- Tumor microenvironment (e.g. CD8+ infiltration, transcriptomic GSEA in patent filings)

### Pete Wilds (POI)

- Joined **2015**; rose to **SVP, Cancer Biology** (not Head of Discovery Biology — adjacent to Early Discovery under Gill).
- Built in vitro biology underpinning tri-complex RAS(ON) platform; screening for early programs (RMC-4630, RMC-5552 lineage).
- **Background:** Cornell BA Biology; UC Berkeley PhD (Marqusee); postdoc Wells UCSF; DuPont industrial biochemistry.
- **Implication for comp bio:** Values mechanistic rigor, integrated biochemistry/cell biology, translational choice of inhibitor + companion.

### Matt Holderfield (POI)

- **Exec. Director, Cancer Cell & Systems Biology** (promoted from Sr. Director, 2021–2024).
- Co-author on flagship [Nature 2024 RAS(ON) multi-selective](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07205-6) paper (RMC-7977 tool → RMC-6236 clinical analog).
- **Focus:** RAS oncogene signaling, MAPK pathway, preclinical tumor models, resistance and combination strategies.
- **Implication for comp bio:** Systems-level thinking — cell line/xenograft data integration, pathway suppression depth/durability, combination rationale.
